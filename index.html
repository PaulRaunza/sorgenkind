Climate adaptation, applied in Vienna

One of the main problems that could be identified is that people do not adhere to the new traffic rules. This is a vital problem as a new traffic ruling was the main goal of the Pilotphase.

Analysis

Climate adaptation without a political lead

After the political opposition raised concerns about the Supergrätzl the SPÖ acknowledged their shortcomings and stated that they know about the problems and that they will solve them during the building phase. What is critical however is the definition that the SPÖ seems to have. They view Supergrätzls as simply traffic clamed areas. They think that supergrätzls are already present within Vienna. This brings with it the problem that the only argument that makes something a supergrätzl is traffic reduction, an argument which is hard to debunk without severe research. Further the denieing of the argument would simply remove the status of supergrätzl not have an impact on the situation. It would mean that supergrätzl no longer create traffic reduction but that traffic reduction creates supergrätzls. This creats an situation in which the viennese politicians can portray themselves in whichever way they want.

Changing how we live in our cities is an endorsement that takes political courage. With the announcement of the superillias in barcelona many residents voiced their discomfort, their fears. Now, after their realization, those voices have been replaced by praises.

Similar holds true for vienna. XXX had to fight to change the Mariahilferstraße. Today people can't envision going back to the motoriesed verion. In the Supergrätzl Favoriten people are for the creation of a less trafficed place. But they are angry about the realisation. They are angry about the half hearted rules that do not have any effects other than being present. We need politicians that show courage. Politicians that have a vision, politicians who are not afraid.
